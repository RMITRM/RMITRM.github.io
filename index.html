My Future In The Industry

Everyone has plans for what they'd like for the future, and I am no different. Moving forward, there's one job that has a bit of everything that I like when it comes to IT as a whole...

The Dream Job

Valve Anti-Cheat Software Engineer

The role of an Anti-Cheat Software Engineer involves the development of software counter-measures that are designed to prevent cheating or hacking behaviour in online videogames. The position is one that involves reverse engineering skills necessary to pick apart hacks built around a game engine, in addition to programming skills. This is in order to build safe guards against and enhance the detection of such malicious influence. In addition to these skills, the job necessitates a keen understanding of dynamic link libraries (.dll), excecutable files (.exe) as well as process and memory management; due to these game hacks frequently employing exploits involving the injection of foreign libraries into the executable or the manipulation of the host system's memory.

This job interests me greatly for a variety of reasons, both personally and professionally. Personally, the position is enticing due to the fact that it is at a company that I've always enjoyed the work of; Valve itself is a long standing and respected company, and they have an extremely high standard of expectation from their employees. Professionally, the position is one that involves a constant arms race of development whereby the anti-cheat developers must patch up new holes constantly being poked in their software's defenses. This means that the job is one that is always evolving, an endless competitive puzzle of security versus invasion that challenges the engineer to think of new and creative solutions while always considering the potential impact on the honest end user.

My Project Idea

Overview

When it comes to ideas for projects, there were a few bouncing around in my head. In the end, I decided to go with the idea that I deemed the most practical in terms of development and real world use. My project idea is the development and launch of a nutrition and meal planning application for mobile devices. This application would be designed with the intent of allowing the end user to quickly parse a few tags through it; returning to them a list of potential ingredients for meals that satisfy their criteria while offering good nutritional value and a bit of variety.

Motivation

The motivation for this idea came from a very personal place. As someone that has had to very quickly take their life into their own hands shortly after graduating from secondary education, I have often found myself being caught up on the little necessities of life. Often when considering groceries, I have found myself lost for what I truly want to eat. I also struggle with knowing if I’m reaching nutritional quotas, and can find it difficult to think of new and interesting things to try when on the spot. All too often, this ended with purchasing the same few things I knew I could count on, boring as they may be. Eventually, this led me to the idea of an application that could quickly suggest ingredients based on a few vague inputs; allowing the user to focus on other important parts of life.

Description

The product itself will be a mobile application that can be readily downloaded from a device’s native storefront. Once launched, the user will be able to browse through and select various pre-programmed tags for the kinds of food they crave, or the nutritional values important to them. A few examples of potential tags include:

  • White Meat
  • Gluten Free
  • Carbohydrates
  • Dietary Fibre
  • Whole Grains

Upon being satisfied with the current selection of tags, the user would submit them. Once submitted, the application would return a list of potential related meals that they could peruse for ideas on what ingredients to purchase. Along with information on individual meals, the application would be able to store a “shopping list”, wherein the user is able to store the ingredients of multiple of their previously selected meals for ease of reference when grocery shopping.

In addition to the simple display and storage of meals, the application would have the ability to inform the user if it believes they are neglecting any particular kind of nutrition with their current list. This information would be based on a user defined value for how long their groceries are intended to last, from a day to multiple weeks. This setting, along with others, would be stored in an optional part of the application for more advanced users. Within this space necessary options should be included in the end product, such as the ability for the user to define specific and permanent dietary needs; this would include health concerns such as coeliac and allergies, ruling out problematic foods and suggesting more appropriate options as necessary.

Due to the nature of the application requiring it to be capable of retrieving items based on a tag system, it will need some form of database to retrieve these from. This database should be something openly available for the end user to browse, should they choose to. While browsing this database of food items, the user will be capable of marking specific ingredients as favourites. Any item marked as a favourite will be weighted higher when it comes to recommendations, with related foods also being recommended more often.

Tools and Technology

The successful realisation of this project will hinge on the use of a mobile multi-platform application development suite, of which there are several. The ideal candidate for this is React Native, an open-source Java driven suite already in use globally. React Native allows the developer to write a single codebase containing platform specific code, such that a single project can encompass native support for both iOS and Android simultaneously. In addition to this, while not necessary, access to both Android and iOS devices for practical testing would be a boon towards the development process.

Skills Required

The skills required for the development of this application will largely revolve around the learning of a cross-platform development tool for mobile devices. The majority of these development tools use some combination of Java and CSS/HTML, and such a thorough understanding of these languages will be necessary. The first step toward attaining the skills necessary for this project will be picking which cross-development platform will be used, followed by further study of the language used therein. As I am currently studying the Introduction to Programming subject with RMIT, my grasp of Java will naturally further as time passes.

Outcome

The successful launch of the product will be once it has been shipped to iOS and Android storefronts for download. Once available, the application will offer the ability for any user to quickly and easily determine what kind of meals they wish to plan and why. It should also open the door for users to explore foods they otherwise may not consider; the perfect environment to learn for less culinary inclined individuals. Bachelors, young adults freshly fending for themselves, and anyone that wants to save some money and eat healthier by avoiding fast food will have an easy to access way of saving the time and headache of grocery shopping.
